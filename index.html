Profile

I received my B. E. degree in Computer Engineering from Kasetsart University, Thailand, in 2010. Thereafter, I pursued Master and Doctoral degrees at Osaka University, Japan, under Prof. Toshimitsu Ushio in the research group of complex systems. I was fortunate to receive Monbukagakusho (MEXT) scholarships during my graduate studies. My graduate studies research mainly focused on optimal supervisory control of quantitative discrete event systems modeled by weighted automata.

After complete my Ph. D., I have been appointed as a postdoctoral researcher at the same research group. My researches focus on the control of discrete event systems.

I am is currently a postdoctoral researcher at National Institute of Informatics (NII), working in ERATO MMSD -- Metamathematics for Systems Design.
